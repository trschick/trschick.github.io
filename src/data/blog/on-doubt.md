I have resolved to demolish everything, and to begin again from the foundations. For I have come to realize that many of the opinions I have held since my youth are false, and that what I have built upon them is doubtful and uncertain.

I will therefore make a serious and unimpeded effort to destroy all my former opinions. To this end, it will not be necessary for me to show that they are all false; that would be an impossible task. But since reason now persuades me that I ought to withhold my assent no less carefully from opinions that are not completely certain and indubitable than from those that are patently false, it will be sufficient for their rejection if I can find in each of them some reason for doubt.

I will suppose, then, that everything I see is false. I will believe that none of those things that my deceitful memory represents to me ever existed. I have no senses. Body, shape, extension, movement, and place are chimeras. What then will be true? Perhaps just the single fact that nothing is certain.
