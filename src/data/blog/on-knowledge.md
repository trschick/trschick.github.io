You say that you know many things. But what is this thing you call knowledge? Is it merely true belief? Or is it something more?

A man may have a true belief, and yet not have knowledge. For he may have heard it from another, and not have examined it for himself.

Is knowledge, then, justified true belief? But what is justification? And how can we be sure that our beliefs are truly justified?

Perhaps true knowledge is not of this world. Perhaps it is a memory of a time when our souls were one with the gods.

Let us inquire into this matter. For I know only one thing, and that is that I know nothing.
