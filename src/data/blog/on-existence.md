But I have persuaded myself that there is absolutely nothing in the world: no sky, no earth, no minds, no bodies. Is it then the case that I too do not exist? But if I persuaded myself of something, then I certainly existed.

But there is a deceiver of supreme power and cunning who is deliberately and constantly deceiving me. In that case, I too undoubtedly exist, if he is deceiving me; and let him deceive me as much as he can, he will never bring it about that I am nothing so long as I think that I am something.

So that after having reflected well and carefully examined all things, we must come to the definite conclusion that this proposition: "I am, I exist," is necessarily true each time that I pronounce it, or that I mentally conceive it.
