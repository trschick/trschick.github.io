Pray tell, what is this thing you call virtue? Is it one thing, or many? Can it be taught, or is it a gift from the gods?

Some say that courage is a virtue, and that temperance is a virtue, and that justice is a virtue. But are these not all different things? And if they are different, how can they all be virtue?

Perhaps virtue is a kind of knowledge. The knowledge of good and evil. If a man knows what is good, will he not do it? And if he knows what is evil, will he not avoid it?

But if virtue is knowledge, then it can be taught. And if it can be taught, then there must be teachers of virtue. But who are these teachers? And what do they teach?

Let us examine this question together. For the unexamined life is not worth living.
