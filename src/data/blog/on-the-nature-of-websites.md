**Socrates:** Tell me, Glaucon, have you ever considered the nature of these... "websites" that the young people are so fond of?

**Glaucon:** I have, Socrates. They seem to be a collection of words and images, displayed on a glowing screen.

**Socrates:** But is that their true nature? Are they merely the sum of their parts? Or is there something more to them? Is there a "Form" of a website, an ideal that all websites aspire to?

**Glaucon:** I am not sure I follow, Socrates.

**Socrates:** Consider this website you are looking at now. It is but a shadow on the wall of a cave, a fleeting copy of a more perfect, eternal website. This perfect website is not made of code and pixels, but of pure reason and truth. It is the Form of the website, and it is only by turning away from the shadows on the screen and looking towards the light of reason that we can ever hope to apprehend it.

**Glaucon:** So, you are saying that all these websites are just imperfect copies of a perfect, ideal website?

**Socrates:** Precisely. And it is the task of the philosopher, and the web developer, to strive to create a website that is as close to the perfect Form as possible. A website that is not just a collection of words and images, but a reflection of truth and beauty.
